Case Study

Falabella

Legacy Commerce Modernization - Transforming 135+ years of retail heritage with modern cloud-native architecture on Azure.

Overview

Falabella, a Latin American retail giant with 135+ years of history, partnered with NULogic to migrate from its outdated Oracle ATG platform to a modern, cloud-native architecture on Azure. This transformation enabled one of the region's largest department store chains to overcome critical infrastructure challenges and embrace the agility needed for contemporary e-commerce demands.

Services Delivered

  • Cloud Engineering and DevOps
  • Monitoring and Support
  • Cloud-native infrastructure using Azure and Docker
  • "Environment as Code" approach for development workflows

The Challenge

Falabella faced multiple critical obstacles with its aging ATG system that threatened its competitive position in the rapidly evolving e-commerce landscape:

Outdated Technology

The legacy framework lacked the flexibility and agility needed for contemporary e-commerce demands, limiting innovation and speed to market.

Infrastructure Issues

Configuration files remained unversioned on servers, creating significant operational risks and making it difficult to track changes or roll back problematic deployments.

Knowledge Silos

Critical architectural expertise was concentrated with few individuals, creating dependency risks and bottlenecks in development and operations.

Rising Costs

Maintaining the old infrastructure proved expensive and resource-intensive compared to modern cloud alternatives, impacting the bottom line without delivering commensurate value.

The Solution

NULogic implemented a comprehensive modernization strategy that addressed each challenge while minimizing disruption to ongoing operations:

  • Cloud-native infrastructure using Azure and Docker for lower environments, providing scalable and reliable foundation for modern e-commerce operations
  • "Environment as Code" approach enabling teams to create development environments per sprint, eliminating configuration drift and improving developer productivity
  • Iterative execution with frequent validation and collaborative planning, ensuring alignment with business goals and minimizing risk
  • DevOps automation with continuous deployment pipelines, accelerating release cycles and improving code quality
  • Security features including encryption and auditing for compliance, protecting sensitive customer and business data
