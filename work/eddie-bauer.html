Case Study

From Legacy to Cloud: Eddie Bauer's Digital Commerce Makeover with MACH Architecture and AWS Infrastructure

Transforming a 100+ year old outdoor brand's digital commerce with modern MACH architecture on AWS.

Region

U.S.

Website

eddiebauer.com

Client Since

2019

Client Overview

Eddie Bauer is a 100+ year old outdoor brand providing rugged, high-quality gear including performance outerwear, apparel, footwear, and accessories. Products available through their website and 200+ retail locations across U.S., Canada, Germany, Japan, and international markets.

The Challenge

The company operated on an outdated, monolithic ATG framework that created significant limitations:

Legacy Technology Restrictions

ATG framework prevented agile updates and caused performance bottlenecks

Scalability Requirements

Needed modern, modular architecture supporting quick scaling and third-party integrations

Payment Processing Failures

Order authorization issues post-MAO mismatches disrupted fulfillment workflows

Infrastructure Complexity

Legacy configurations increased maintenance overhead and operational costs

The Solution

MACH Architecture Implementation

Replaced monolithic ATG system with Node.js, React, GraphQL, Python, MongoDB, and AWS microservices, enabling weekly production releases with module-level deployments.

AWS Infrastructure Migration

  • Deployed AWS CloudFront for faster global content delivery
  • Optimized server performance and page load times during peak shopping periods
  • Integrated WAF with CloudFront to block malicious bot traffic targeting GraphQL endpoints

DevOps & Cost Optimization

  • Switched Lambda processors from x86 to ARM64 architecture, reducing compute costs approximately 32%
  • Eliminated redundant logs, Lambda executions, and MongoDB collections

Monitoring & Analytics

  • Implemented DataDog and GTM for real-time event tracking, error monitoring, and performance analytics
  • Deployed EB Toolkit providing QA and business users deployment and testing visibility
